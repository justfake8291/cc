1. *Amazon EC2 (Elastic Compute Cloud)*:
   - *Case Study*: Netflix relies heavily on Amazon EC2 for its streaming service. By utilizing EC2's scalability and reliability, Netflix can handle massive traffic spikes during peak hours without compromising the quality of their service. They can easily scale their infrastructure up or down based on demand, optimizing costs and performance.
   - *Key Features*: On-demand scalability, reliability, cost-effectiveness, extensive selection of instance types, and integration with other AWS services.

2. *Microsoft Azure*:
   - *Case Study*: Boeing, one of the world's largest aerospace companies, leverages Microsoft Azure for various purposes, including data analytics, simulation, and digital transformation initiatives. Azure's cloud computing services help Boeing analyze massive amounts of data generated during aircraft manufacturing and flight operations, leading to improved efficiency and innovation.
   - *Key Features*: Hybrid cloud capabilities, extensive compliance certifications, AI and machine learning services, and seamless integration with Microsoft products like Office 365 and Dynamics 365.

3. *Google Cloud Platform (GCP)*:
   - *Case Study*: Spotify, a popular music streaming service, utilizes Google Cloud Platform to deliver personalized recommendations to its users. GCP's advanced data analytics and machine learning capabilities enable Spotify to process vast amounts of user data in real-time, providing personalized playlists and recommendations tailored to each user's preferences.
   - *Key Features*: Data analytics and machine learning services, global network infrastructure, Kubernetes-based container orchestration, and strong emphasis on sustainability and environmental responsibility.

  Conclusion:
In conclusion, Amazon EC2, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud Platform are three leading cloud service providers, each offering a wide range of services and features tailored to meet the diverse needs of businesses.
